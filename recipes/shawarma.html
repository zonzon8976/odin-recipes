Shawarma is a delicious and well-loved street food known for its rich, juicy fillings wrapped in soft flatbread. This flavorful wrap combines marinated grilled chicken or beef with a creamy garlic sauce, fresh vegetables, and a hint of spice, making it a among food lovers.

Served in pita or tortilla wraps, Shawarma is often paired with cheese, coleslaw, or fries for an extra layer of indulgence. Whether enjoyed as a snack or a full meal, Nigerian Shawarma delivers a burst of bold, savory flavors with every bite.

  1. Marinate the protein: In a bowl, mix garlic, ginger, onion powder, black pepper, paprika, olive oil, vinegar, bouillon powder, and salt. Coat the chicken or beef with this marinade and let it sit for at least 2 hours (or overnight for deeper flavor).

  2. Grill the meat: Heat a pan or grill and cook the marinated protein on medium heat until tender and slightly charred. Slice into thin strips.

  3. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, mix mayonnaise, ketchup, hot sauce, and garlic sauce for a creamy, spicy flavor.

  4. Assemble the Shawarma: Lay the flatbread or tortilla on a clean surface. Spread a generous layer of the sauce, then add sliced meat, cucumbers, shredded cabbage, carrots, and onions.

  5. Wrap it up: Fold one side of the bread over the filling, tuck in the sides, and roll tightly.

  6. Toast the Shawarma: Place the wrapped Shawarma on a heated dry pan and toast for 1-2 minutes per side until golden brown and slightly crispy.

  7. Serve & Enjoy: Cut in half and serve warm with extra sauce, fries, or a cold drink.
